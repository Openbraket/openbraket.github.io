Why Quantum Computing for Finance?

Certain financial workloads exhibit scaling challenges where quantum algorithms offer new computational pathways.

1. High-Dimensional State Representation

Quantum systems represent portfolio configurations in Hilbert spaces of dimension 2n using n qubits, enabling parallel amplitude encoding rather than classical data compression.

2. Quadratic Monte Carlo Speedup (Asymptotic)

Quantum Amplitude Estimation achieves O(1/ε) query complexity compared to classical Monte Carlo’s O(1/ε²). Practical performance on near-term hardware remains an active research area.

3. Natural Optimization Landscape

Portfolio optimization with cardinality, transaction costs, and ESG constraints forms NP-hard combinatorial problems. QAOA provides a variational heuristic framework for exploring such landscapes.

4. Emerging Hardware Capability

Contemporary quantum processors report high single-qubit gate fidelities under benchmark conditions, while multi-qubit noise limits circuit depth. This motivates hybrid quantum-classical workflows.

5. Regulatory & Computational Pressure

Basel III / FRTB stress testing imposes substantial recurring computational demands on large financial institutions, motivating exploration of algorithmic acceleration.
